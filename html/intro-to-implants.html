a permanent, natural looking solution

Missing a tooth, or multiple teeth, can be a stressful experience. Our patients often talk to us about the challenges associated with missing teeth and a changing bite pattern, such as a new diet, difficulty pronouncing certain words or phrases and an overall sense of self-consciousness. Those with long-term tooth loss also experience the detrimental effects of bone loss, which can cause the teeth to shift or further tooth loss as the bone degrades. For a permanent, natural-looking solution, our doctors offer dental implants.

Your TeethToday Options

Dental implants blend seamlessly into the smile to replace missing teeth with screw-like implants that adhere with the jaw bone. As you bite and chew with the attached dental crown, the implant provides the same bone stimulation as a natural tooth root, which keeps your bone levels healthy and protects the integrity of your smile.

Single dental implant: If you are missing even one tooth, your oral health is at risk. Without a tooth to stimulate the jaw bone, bone loss begins to occur which can lead to shifting of other teeth or increased risk of infection. We can replace your missing tooth with a natural-looking implant the same day.

Multiple dental implants: Missing more than one tooth is common and the issue should be addressed immediately. Unlike a bridge which relies on surrounding teeth to mask the gap, our TeethToday implants provide a healthy, more natural alternative to bridges.

Full arch dental implants: If you have dentures and are looking for a more stable, healthier alternative, we can complete your smile in one visit. This option uses a number of dental implants (as few as four) surgically placed in the jaw bone to support a custom, natural-looking prosthesis. There are no pastes or need to worry about slipping dentures; this option is designed to stay in place.

Ceramic Dental Implants Offer an Attractive Alternative to Metal

Our team also offers StraumannÂ® PURE Ceramic Implants as a metal-free alternative. This unique and highly aesthetic solution works the same as traditional titanium implants, but is made from zirconium-dioxide ceramic and ivory-colored materials for a more natural look. Introducing Robotic Assisted Implant Surgeries

In staying current with the latest in dental technology, our doctors are some of the first in the country to offer robotic dental implant surgeries in our office. The FDA-cleared Yomi system is the first robotically assisted dental system that aides our doctors in planning, adjusting and performing implant surgeries in a minimally-invasive and more accurate manner. The technology allows our doctors to be in control at all times and ensures your implants are placed exactly where planned. We are excited to introduce this technology and provide an even better patient experience.
